# Fluent 2 Design Principles

Design principles serve as foundational values that guide decision-making and help product designers solve problems. Microsoft's Fluent principles are rooted in understanding how customers navigate the world and beliefs about empowering people to accomplish more.

## 1. Natural on every platform

Experiences should adapt to different devices and build on familiar patterns that users already understand.

- **Functional**: Layouts that respond to various screen sizes and platform awareness enable reusing native components and patterns approximately 80% of the time, allowing focus on distinctive experiences.
- **Emotional**: Experiences are intuitive and expected, creating a feeling of reliability and trust.

## 2. Built for focus

Users want to maintain momentum. Experiences should inspire action that is simple and seamless.

- **Functional**: Technology should communicate and perform effectively without hindering users' desired actions.
- **Emotional**: Reduced visual clutter and noise help people stay centered, calm, and confident.

## 3. One for all, all for one

Experiences should consider diverse perspectives and abilities for universal benefit.

- **Functional**: Including varied perspectives early in design creates better solutions and encourages creative problem-solving.
- **Emotional**: Inclusion fosters belonging.

## 4. Unmistakably Microsoft

Experiences should feel cohesive across Microsoft products and be easily recognizable.

- **Functional**: Focus on signature experiences across color, sound, illustration, and icons increases brand recognition.
- **Emotional**: "A little personality goes a long way."
