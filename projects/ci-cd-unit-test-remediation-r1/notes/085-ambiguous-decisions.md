## Top patterns observed

### DELETE-judged tests
1. **Record equality tests** — 9 of 18 (50%). Generated C# code; compiler enforces. Most prevalent scaffolding pattern.
2. **Placeholders for future code** — 4 of 18 (Sessions/Telemetry rows + manual-integration checklist). `[Fact(Skip = "Reactivate when X is implemented")]` with `Assert.Fail`. These ship as test debt and never get reactivated.
3. **Empty bodies / comment-only "tests"** — 2 of 18. Often have a comment explaining why the test can't be written.
4. **"No assertion needed" tests** — 2 of 18. Body relies on absence-of-exception from a strict mock or claims "compile-time test".
5. **Fragile mirror tests on internal cardinality** — 1 of 18. `Assert.Single(SupportedX)` when `Assert.Contains(ExpectedX, SupportedX)` already exists.

### KEEP-upgraded tests
1. **ADR-015 telemetry/PII contracts** — 24 of 48 (50%). Mature suite using `AssertTelemetryRespectsAdr015` helper. Real security regression protection.
2. **Wire-format contracts (JSON discriminators, SSE event types)** — 5 of 48. Consumer-visible contracts.
3. **Boundary-condition / branch-coverage assertions on business logic** — 3 of 48 (SignalEvaluation thresholds). Each protects a real branch.
4. **ADR-032 Null-Object quiet-semantics safety** — 4 of 48 (TodoSync + Membership). Kill-switch safety invariants.
5. **Invariants on event/data ordering** — 3 of 48 (WorkingDocumentTools EndIsAlwaysLast). Client-facing contract.

The heuristic-pass over-classified telemetry tests as AMBIGUOUS because they end in `Verify(...).Times.Never())` patterns or use mocks heavily — but reading the test body shows the assertion IS behavioral (no-leak property of the captured log scan).
