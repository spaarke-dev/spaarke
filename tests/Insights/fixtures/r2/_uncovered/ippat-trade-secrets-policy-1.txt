1. PURPOSE

This Trade Secrets Protection Policy ("Policy") establishes Helios Quantum
Compute, Inc.'s ("HQC") procedures for identifying, marking, protecting, and
maintaining the confidentiality of HQC's trade secrets, in compliance with
the Defend Trade Secrets Act of 2016 (18 U.S.C. § 1836) and applicable state
trade-secret laws.

2. SCOPE

This Policy applies to all HQC employees, contractors, consultants, board
members, and other parties having access to HQC's confidential information.

3. DEFINITIONS

   "Trade Secret" means any information that (a) derives independent economic
   value, actual or potential, from not being generally known to, and not
   being readily ascertainable through proper means by, another person who
   can obtain economic value from its disclosure or use, and (b) is the
   subject of efforts that are reasonable under the circumstances to maintain
   its secrecy.

   "Trade Secret Asset Register" or "Register" means HQC's master inventory
   of identified Trade Secrets, maintained by the General Counsel.

4. IDENTIFICATION OF TRADE SECRETS

Trade Secrets at HQC include, but are not limited to:

   (a) Internal R&D research data, including thermal-management feedback
       control algorithms and Kalman-filter tuning parameters that are NOT
       disclosed in pending or issued patents;
   (b) Manufacturing process parameters for piezoelectric microvalve
       integration with stacked accelerator arrays;
   (c) Customer pricing models, business strategy, and forward roadmap;
   (d) Source code for HQC's proprietary thermal simulation toolchain
       (not the firmware running on shipped products); and
   (e) Vendor relationship terms not publicly disclosed.

5. PROCEDURES FOR PROTECTION

   5.1 Identification and Registration. The General Counsel maintains the
       Trade Secret Asset Register, updated quarterly with input from the
       CTO and the Director of R&D.

   5.2 Access Control. Trade Secrets shall be stored in access-controlled
       systems with role-based permissions managed by IT under guidance
       from the General Counsel.

   5.3 Marking. Documents and digital files containing Trade Secrets shall
       be marked "HQC TRADE SECRET" with a brief description of the asset
       category.

   5.4 Employee Education. All employees receive Trade Secret training at
       onboarding and annually thereafter. Training records are maintained
       by HR for a period of seven (7) years.

   5.5 NDA Coverage. Trade Secrets may not be disclosed to any third party
       without (a) a written Non-Disclosure Agreement reviewed and approved
       by the General Counsel, and (b) only such disclosure as is required
       for the legitimate business purpose for which the third party is
       engaged.

6. PROCEDURES FOR INCIDENT RESPONSE

If an actual or suspected unauthorized disclosure of a Trade Secret is
identified, the following procedure applies:

   6.1 The individual identifying the incident shall report immediately to
       the General Counsel and, if appropriate, the Chief Information
       Security Officer.

   6.2 The General Counsel shall convene a Trade Secret Incident Response
       Team within twenty-four (24) hours, consisting of: General Counsel
       (lead), CTO, CISO, Director of HR, and outside counsel as needed.

   6.3 The Incident Response Team shall (a) assess the scope and impact of
       the incident, (b) implement immediate containment measures, (c)
       determine appropriate notifications (legal, regulatory, customer,
       investor), and (d) coordinate any necessary legal action, including
       potential filings under the Defend Trade Secrets Act.

7. ANNUAL REVIEW

This Policy and the Trade Secret Asset Register shall be reviewed at least
annually by the General Counsel, with material updates approved by the
Board of Directors' Risk and Compliance Committee.
