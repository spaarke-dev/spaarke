REMARKS

The Applicant respectfully submits this Response to the Non-Final Office Action
mailed March 24, 2026. Pursuant to 37 CFR 1.111, this Response addresses each

I. AMENDMENTS TO THE CLAIMS

   Claim 1 (currently amended):
      A photonic routing structure for low-insertion-loss wavelength-selective
      routing, comprising: a silicon-nitride waveguide array; and at least one
      asymmetric ring resonator coupled to the waveguide array, wherein the
      asymmetric ring resonator has a first coupling region and a second
      coupling region, the first and second coupling regions having a
      coupling-region asymmetry ratio of between 1.4 and 2.6, *AND WHEREIN
      THE ASYMMETRIC RING RESONATOR FURTHER COMPRISES AN INTRA-CAVITY
      DISPERSION-COMPENSATION ELEMENT POSITIONED BETWEEN THE FIRST AND SECOND
      COUPLING REGIONS AND CONFIGURED TO REDUCE GROUP-DELAY DISPERSION WITHIN
      THE RING TO LESS THAN 5 ps/nm AT A WAVELENGTH OF 1550 nm*.

   (Words in CAPITALS are additions; no deletions made.)

   Support for the added limitations is found at specification paragraphs
   [0089]–[0094] and Figs. 6A–6C as originally filed.

   Claims 7, 20–24, 25, and 26 are amended as set forth in the marked-up claim

II. ARGUMENTS RESPONDING TO § 103 REJECTIONS

   A. The Park-Rajagopalan combination does not teach or suggest the
      dispersion-compensation element added to amended claim 1.

      Neither Park nor Rajagopalan discloses an intra-cavity dispersion
      compensation element positioned between the coupling regions of an
      asymmetric ring resonator. Park is silent regarding dispersion
      compensation. Rajagopalan, while teaching asymmetric coupling for
      wavelength selectivity, expressly relies on the natural dispersion
      characteristics of the ring (Rajagopalan, paragraph [0079]: "additional
      dispersion compensation is unnecessary for the disclosed wavelength
      ranges"). One of ordinary skill, reading Rajagopalan, would have been
      taught AWAY from incorporating an additional dispersion-compensation
      element.

   B. The combination lacks a reasonable expectation of success under
      KSR-style obviousness analysis.

      Even if one were to combine Park and Rajagopalan as suggested by the
      Examiner, the resulting structure would not provide the insertion-loss
      reduction required for the claimed application range. The Singh
      reference cited by the Examiner at item G.4 in fact demonstrates that
      asymmetric ring resonators without intra-cavity dispersion compensation
      exhibit insertion losses of 0.45 dB or greater at the relevant operating
      wavelengths (Singh, Table II) — well above the 0.18 dB threshold
      Applicant teaches as enabling for low-loss routing applications
      (specification, paragraph [0024]).

III. ARGUMENTS RESPONDING TO § 112(b) REJECTIONS

   Amended claim 7 now recites "a coupling profile having a spatial period of
   200 nm and a period-to-period variance of less than 3%," providing
   quantitative bounds in compliance with 35 U.S.C. § 112(b).

IV. ARGUMENT RESPONDING TO § 101 REJECTION

   Amended claim 25 now recites "a non-transitory computer-readable storage
   medium storing instructions executable by a processor to perform the
   method of claim 1." This amendment places claim 25 squarely within the
   "manufacture" statutory category and complies with Examiner guidance under
   MPEP § 2106 regarding tangible-medium claims.
