1. PROJECT OVERVIEW

This Patent Prosecution Project Plan ("PPP") sets the strategic prosecution
roadmap for the patent family rooted in U.S. Provisional Application
63/598,142 (filed Nov 3, 2025) and U.S. Non-Provisional Application
18/734,521 (filed Mar 11, 2026). Subject matter: adaptive thermal management
in high-density compute fabrics using fluid-dynamic feedback control.

3. STRATEGIC OBJECTIVES

   a) Build a defensive patent thicket around the core thermal-management
      method to deter competitive products from major accelerator OEMs.
   b) Position the family for licensing into the data-center cooling
      sub-segment (target licensees: Tier-1 hyperscalers and merchant cooling
      vendors).
   c) Secure broad jurisdictional coverage in the top 5 hyperscaler-operating
      jurisdictions: US, EU (EP), JP, KR, CN.

4. KEY PROSECUTION RISKS AND MITIGATIONS

   4.1 Subject-matter eligibility (US § 101). The state-space-model
       limitations could attract a § 101 software-eligibility rejection.
       Mitigation: amended claims include explicit hardware actuator
       (piezoelectric microvalve) and millisecond control-horizon limitation
       (see independent claim 1 as filed).

   4.2 Prior-art landscape (US § 103). Singh et al. (IEEE Photonics Journal,
       June 2023) discloses comparable feedback-control approaches; the
       distinguishing limitation is the 1 kHz sampling rate and the Kalman
       filter tuning to chip-package thermal time constants. Mitigation:
       maintain detailed inventor declarations supporting non-obviousness
       through experimental data captured in HQC's internal lab notebooks
       LN-2025-187 through LN-2025-203.

   4.3 Continuation strategy. Filing the CIP must not introduce new matter
       that would impair the parent's priority claim. Mitigation: outside
       counsel will review the CIP specification for new-matter compliance
       before filing.
