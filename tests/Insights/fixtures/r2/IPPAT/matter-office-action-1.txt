C. CLAIM REJECTIONS — 35 U.S.C. § 103

Claims 1–6, 8–10, and 12–18 are REJECTED under 35 U.S.C. § 103 as being
unpatentable over Park et al. (U.S. Patent No. 11,287,409, "Park") in view of
Rajagopalan (U.S. Pub. No. 2023/0114502 A1, "Rajagopalan").

   Regarding claim 1, Park discloses a photonic routing structure comprising a
   silicon-nitride waveguide array and at least one ring resonator coupled to
   the waveguide array (Park, Figs. 2 and 5; col. 6, lines 18–47). Park does
   not explicitly disclose an asymmetric ring resonator geometry having a
   coupling-region asymmetry ratio of between 1.4 and 2.6. However,
   Rajagopalan teaches asymmetric ring resonator coupling regions for
   wavelength-selective routing in photonic networks (Rajagopalan,
   paragraphs [0058]–[0072]; Fig. 7) and identifies coupling-region asymmetry
   ratios of approximately 1.8 as advantageous for low-insertion-loss routing
   in the C-band.

   It would have been obvious to one of ordinary skill in the art at the time
   the application was filed to modify the photonic routing structure of Park
   to incorporate the asymmetric coupling geometry taught by Rajagopalan in
   order to reduce insertion loss in wavelength-selective routing applications,
   as expressly taught by Rajagopalan, paragraph [0061].

D. CLAIM REJECTIONS — 35 U.S.C. § 112(b)

Claims 7, 20–24, and 26 are REJECTED under 35 U.S.C. § 112(b) as being
indefinite. The phrase "substantially periodic coupling profile" in claim 7
lacks an antecedent basis sufficient for one of ordinary skill to ascertain
the metes and bounds of the claim. The Examiner suggests amendment to recite a
specific quantitative metric (e.g., "a coupling profile having a spatial
period of P and a period-to-period variance of less than 5%").

E. CLAIM REJECTIONS — 35 U.S.C. § 101

Claim 25 is REJECTED under 35 U.S.C. § 101 as being directed to non-statutory
subject matter, namely a propagating optical signal per se, which does not
fall within any of the four statutory categories of invention.

F. OBJECTIONS TO CLAIMS

Claims 11 and 19 are objected to as being dependent upon a rejected base
