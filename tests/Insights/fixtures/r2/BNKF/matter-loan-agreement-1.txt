CREDIT AND SECURITY AGREEMENT

This Credit and Security Agreement (this "Agreement") is dated as of February
27, 2026 (the "Closing Date"), among:

  BORROWER: Mariner Logistics Group LLC, a Delaware limited liability company
            ("Borrower"), with its principal place of business at 4400 Harbor
            Way, Norfolk, VA 23510;

  GUARANTORS: Mariner Holdings, Inc., a Delaware corporation, and Mariner
              Coastal Operations LLC, a Virginia limited liability company
              (each a "Guarantor" and collectively the "Guarantors");

  ADMINISTRATIVE AGENT: Linden Trust National Bank, N.A., a national banking
                       association, in its capacity as administrative agent
                       for the Lenders ("Administrative Agent"); and

  LENDERS: The lenders party hereto from time to time (each a "Lender" and
           collectively the "Lenders").

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Borrower has requested that the Lenders make available to Borrower a
senior secured term loan facility in the original principal amount of EIGHTY
MILLION DOLLARS ($80,000,000) (the "Term Loan") and a revolving credit
facility in the maximum aggregate commitment amount of TWENTY-FIVE MILLION
DOLLARS ($25,000,000) (the "Revolving Facility");

WHEREAS, the Lenders are willing to make the Term Loan and the Revolving
Facility available to Borrower on the terms and subject to the conditions set
forth herein;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants set forth herein, the
parties agree as follows:

ARTICLE 1 — THE TERM LOAN

1.1 Term Loan Commitment. Subject to the terms and conditions set forth
    herein, each Lender severally agrees to make to Borrower on the Closing
    Date a term loan in the principal amount set forth opposite such Lender's
    name on Schedule 1.1 (its "Term Loan Commitment"). The aggregate principal
    amount of the Term Loan made by all Lenders shall be EIGHTY MILLION
    DOLLARS ($80,000,000).

1.2 Maturity Date. The "Term Loan Maturity Date" shall mean February 27, 2031,
    being five (5) years following the Closing Date.

1.3 Amortization. The Term Loan shall amortize in equal quarterly installments
    in an amount equal to 1.25% of the original principal amount of the Term
    Loan (i.e., $1,000,000 per quarter), commencing on June 30, 2026, with the
    balance due at the Term Loan Maturity Date.

1.4 Interest Rate. The Term Loan shall bear interest at a rate per annum equal
    to, at Borrower's election: (a) the Alternate Base Rate plus 2.25%, or
    (b) the Adjusted Term SOFR Rate plus 3.25%. Interest shall be payable
    quarterly in arrears on each Interest Payment Date.

1.5 Default Rate. Upon and during the continuance of an Event of Default, all
    outstanding Obligations shall bear interest at the otherwise applicable
    rate plus 2.00% (the "Default Rate").

ARTICLE 2 — THE REVOLVING FACILITY

2.1 Revolving Commitments. Each Lender severally agrees to make Revolving
    Loans to Borrower at any time and from time to time during the Revolving
    Availability Period in an aggregate principal amount not to exceed such
    Lender's Revolving Commitment.

2.2 Revolving Availability Period. The "Revolving Availability Period" shall
    commence on the Closing Date and terminate on February 27, 2030.

2.3 Letters of Credit. Borrower may request the Issuing Lender to issue
    Letters of Credit, provided that the aggregate undrawn face amount of all
    Letters of Credit plus all unreimbursed drawings shall not at any time
    exceed FIVE MILLION DOLLARS ($5,000,000).

ARTICLE 3 — COLLATERAL AND SECURITY

3.1 Grant of Security Interest. As security for the prompt and complete payment
    and performance of the Obligations, Borrower and each Guarantor hereby
    grant to Administrative Agent, for the ratable benefit of the Lenders, a
    continuing first-priority security interest in all of their respective
    right, title, and interest in and to the Collateral, including without
    limitation: (a) all Accounts, (b) all Inventory, (c) all Equipment, (d)
    all General Intangibles, (e) all Deposit Accounts and Securities Accounts,
    (f) all Investment Property, and (g) all Proceeds and products of any of
    the foregoing.

3.2 Mortgage on Norfolk Real Property. Borrower shall execute and deliver to
    Administrative Agent a Mortgage encumbering the Norfolk Facility located
    at 4400 Harbor Way, Norfolk, VA, securing the Obligations.

ARTICLE 4 — CONDITIONS PRECEDENT TO CLOSING (summary)

Customary closing conditions including: legal opinion from Borrower's counsel
(Holwell, Strand & Adichie LLP); insurance certificates with Administrative
Agent as additional insured and loss payee; payoff letters for all Existing
Indebtedness; UCC financing statements filed in all applicable jurisdictions;
delivery of audited 2024 and 2025 financial statements of Borrower; closing
certificate of Borrower's Chief Financial Officer; and a corporate compliance
certificate from each Guarantor.

ARTICLE 5 — FINANCIAL COVENANTS

5.1 Maximum Total Leverage Ratio. Borrower shall maintain a Total Leverage
    Ratio, tested quarterly, of not greater than:
       4.25:1.00  for the period through December 31, 2026
       4.00:1.00  for the period through December 31, 2027
       3.75:1.00  thereafter

5.2 Minimum Fixed Charge Coverage Ratio. Borrower shall maintain a Fixed
    Charge Coverage Ratio, tested quarterly, of not less than 1.20:1.00.

5.3 Minimum Liquidity. Borrower shall maintain Liquidity (defined as
    unrestricted cash plus undrawn Revolving Commitments) of at least
    Eight Million Dollars ($8,000,000) at all times.

ARTICLE 6 — REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES (summary)

Customary representations regarding organization, authority, no-conflict,
compliance with laws (including OFAC and Anti-Corruption Laws), absence of
litigation other than that disclosed on Schedule 6.7, environmental compliance,
ERISA matters, and the security interest priority described in Article 3.

ARTICLE 7 — EVENTS OF DEFAULT

The following constitute Events of Default: (a) non-payment of principal when
due, with no cure period; (b) non-payment of interest when due, subject to a
five (5) Business Day cure period; (c) breach of any financial covenant in
Article 5; (d) breach of any other covenant uncured for thirty (30) days after
written notice; (e) bankruptcy or insolvency events; (f) cross-default to
Material Indebtedness in excess of Three Million Dollars ($3,000,000); and
(g) change of control as defined in Schedule 7(g).

ARTICLE 8 — GOVERNING LAW; JURISDICTION

8.1 Governing Law. This Agreement and the other Loan Documents shall be
    governed by, and construed in accordance with, the laws of the State of
    New York, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles.

8.2 Venue. The parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of
    the State of New York sitting in New York County and the federal courts
    of the United States for the Southern District of New York.

executed as of the date first written above.
