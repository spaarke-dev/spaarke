MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement (this "Agreement") is entered into as of
January 22, 2026 (the "Effective Date") by and between Lumen Cascade Robotics,
Inc., a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business at 2200 Tech
Boulevard, Mountain View, CA 94043 ("Lumen Cascade"), and Brightwater Capital
Advisors LLC, a Massachusetts limited liability company with its principal
place of business at 100 Federal Street, Floor 27, Boston, MA 02110
("Brightwater"). Each of Lumen Cascade and Brightwater is a "Party" and
collectively the "Parties."

RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Parties are exploring a possible business relationship, which may
include a strategic minority investment by Brightwater in Lumen Cascade (the
"Potential Transaction"); and

WHEREAS, in connection with the evaluation of the Potential Transaction, each
Party may disclose to the other certain Confidential Information (as defined
below);

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants set forth herein, the
Parties agree as follows:

1. DEFINITIONS.

   "Confidential Information" means any non-public information that a Party
   (the "Disclosing Party") furnishes to the other Party (the "Receiving Party")
   in connection with the Potential Transaction, whether oral, written,
   electronic, or visual, and whether or not specifically marked as
   confidential. Confidential Information includes business strategy, financial
   information, product roadmaps, customer lists, technical specifications,
   source code, and the existence and terms of this Agreement.

   "Representatives" means a Party's directors, officers, employees, advisors
   (including attorneys, accountants, and consultants), and affiliates who
   have a need to know in connection with the Potential Transaction.

2. CONFIDENTIALITY OBLIGATIONS.

   2.1 Each Receiving Party shall (a) maintain the Confidential Information of
       the Disclosing Party in strict confidence, (b) use at least the same
       degree of care to protect the Confidential Information as the Receiving
       Party uses for its own confidential information of comparable
       sensitivity (and in no event less than reasonable care), and (c) not
       disclose the Confidential Information to any third party other than its
       Representatives, who shall be bound by confidentiality obligations no
       less restrictive than those set forth herein.

   2.2 The Receiving Party may use the Confidential Information solely for the
       purpose of evaluating the Potential Transaction and not for any other
       purpose, including without limitation any competitive or commercial
       purpose.

3. EXCEPTIONS.

   The obligations of confidentiality and non-use set forth in Section 2 shall
   not apply to information that:

      (a) is or becomes generally available to the public through no breach
          of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;
      (b) was lawfully in the Receiving Party's possession without an
          obligation of confidentiality prior to disclosure by the Disclosing
          Party;
      (c) is lawfully obtained from a third party not under an obligation of
          confidentiality; or
      (d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without reference
          to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information.

4. COMPELLED DISCLOSURE.

   If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or legal process to
   disclose Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall, to the extent
   permitted by law, provide the Disclosing Party with prompt prior written
   notice and reasonable cooperation to enable the Disclosing Party to seek a
   protective order or other appropriate remedy.

5. TERM AND TERMINATION.

   This Agreement shall remain in effect for a period of two (2) years from
   the Effective Date, unless earlier terminated by mutual written agreement.
   The confidentiality and non-use obligations in Section 2 shall survive for
   three (3) years following the Effective Date.

6. RETURN OR DESTRUCTION.

   Upon written request of the Disclosing Party or upon termination of this
   Agreement, the Receiving Party shall, at the Disclosing Party's election,
   return or destroy all Confidential Information in its possession and certify
   such return or destruction in writing.

7. NO LICENSE; NO REPRESENTATIONS.

   No license, ownership interest, or other right in or to any Confidential
   Information is granted or implied by this Agreement. The Disclosing Party
   makes no representations or warranties regarding the accuracy or
   completeness of any Confidential Information.

8. INJUNCTIVE RELIEF.

   The Parties acknowledge that money damages may be inadequate for breach of
   this Agreement and that the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek
   equitable relief, including injunctive relief, in addition to all other
   remedies available at law or in equity.

9. GOVERNING LAW; JURISDICTION.

   This Agreement shall be governed by Delaware law without regard to its
   conflict-of-laws principles. Any dispute arising hereunder shall be brought
   exclusively in the state or federal courts located in New Castle County,
   Delaware.

10. MISCELLANEOUS.

    This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with
    respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior
    communications. This Agreement may not be amended except in a writing
    signed by both Parties. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts,
    each of which shall be deemed an original.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the Parties have executed this Mutual Non-Disclosure
Agreement as of the Effective Date.
