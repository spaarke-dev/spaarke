ASSET PURCHASE AGREEMENT

THIS ASSET PURCHASE AGREEMENT (this "Agreement") is made and entered into as of
February 5, 2026 (the "Effective Date"), by and among:

  (i)   STERLING ARC MEDICAL INSTRUMENTS, INC., a Delaware corporation with its
        principal place of business at 770 Innovation Boulevard, Wilmington, DE
        19801 ("Seller");

  (ii)  CRESCENT DELTA SURGICAL HOLDINGS LLC, a Nevada limited liability company
        with its principal place of business at 1100 Sandhill Drive, Reno, NV
        89509 ("Buyer"); and

  (iii) HENRIK STERLING, an individual residing in the Commonwealth of
        Massachusetts (the "Founder Shareholder").

Seller, Buyer, and the Founder Shareholder are each a "Party" and collectively
the "Parties."

R E C I T A L S

WHEREAS, Seller designs, manufactures, and distributes proprietary minimally
invasive surgical instruments under the "ArcCutter" product line (the
"Business");

WHEREAS, Seller desires to sell, and Buyer desires to purchase, substantially
all of the assets of the Business on the terms and conditions set forth herein;

WHEREAS, the Founder Shareholder owns of record 100% of the issued and
outstanding capital stock of Seller and is joining this Agreement to make the
specific representations and covenants set forth in Article VII;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements set
forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and
sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

ARTICLE I — PURCHASE AND SALE

1.1 Purchase and Sale of Assets. Subject to the terms hereof, at the Closing
    Seller shall sell, convey, assign, transfer, and deliver to Buyer, and
    Buyer shall purchase from Seller, free and clear of all Liens (other than
    Permitted Liens), all right, title, and interest of Seller in and to the
    Purchased Assets, including without limitation: (a) all Intellectual
    Property listed on Schedule 1.1(a) (the "Transferred IP"), (b) all
    inventory and work-in-process, (c) all accounts receivable, (d) the
    Assumed Contracts listed on Schedule 1.1(d), and (e) the Transferred
    Equipment listed on Schedule 1.1(e).

1.2 Excluded Assets. The Purchased Assets do NOT include the Excluded Assets
    listed on Schedule 1.2, including Seller's cash, cash equivalents, and the
    Excluded Real Property at 770 Innovation Boulevard.

1.3 Assumed Liabilities; Excluded Liabilities. At Closing, Buyer shall assume
    only the Assumed Liabilities listed on Schedule 1.3. All other liabilities,
    known or unknown, fixed or contingent, are Excluded Liabilities and shall
    remain the sole responsibility of Seller.

ARTICLE II — PURCHASE PRICE; DEAL STRUCTURE

2.1 Purchase Price. The aggregate purchase price for the Purchased Assets shall
    be SIXTY-EIGHT MILLION SEVEN HUNDRED THOUSAND DOLLARS ($68,700,000) (the
    "Purchase Price"), payable as follows at Closing:

      (a) Sixty-One Million Two Hundred Thousand Dollars ($61,200,000) in cash;
      (b) A non-interest-bearing Promissory Note in the principal amount of
          Four Million Five Hundred Thousand Dollars ($4,500,000), maturing
          eighteen (18) months after Closing; and
      (c) Three Million Dollars ($3,000,000) deposited into the Indemnity
          Escrow (the "Indemnity Escrow Amount").

2.2 Earn-Out. As additional consideration, Seller shall be entitled to receive
    an earn-out payment of up to Five Million Dollars ($5,000,000) (the
    "Earn-Out Amount") based on the ArcCutter product line achieving net
    revenues of at least Eighteen Million Dollars ($18,000,000) for the
    twelve-month period ending on the first anniversary of Closing, as set
    forth in Schedule 2.2.

2.3 Working Capital Adjustment. The Purchase Price shall be adjusted at and
    after Closing for actual versus target Net Working Capital, with the
    target set at Four Million One Hundred Thousand Dollars ($4,100,000) and
    the adjustment determined in accordance with Schedule 2.3.

ARTICLE III — CLOSING

3.1 Closing Date. The Closing shall take place electronically on the second
    business day following the satisfaction or waiver of all conditions set
    forth in Article VIII (the "Closing Date").

ARTICLE IV — REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES OF SELLER (summary)

Sections 4.1–4.22 cover organization, authority, no-conflict, capitalization,
financial statements (including audited 2024 and 2025 statements), tax matters,
real property (none Transferred), intellectual property (with detailed
representations re: the Transferred IP), material contracts, employee matters,
benefit plans, ERISA matters, environmental matters, regulatory compliance
(including FDA 510(k) clearance for the ArcCutter), and absence of changes.

ARTICLE V — REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES OF BUYER

Customary buyer representations regarding organization, authority, sufficient
funds (with executed equity commitment from Crescent Delta's parent fund), and
absence of disqualifying litigation.

ARTICLE VIII — CONDITIONS PRECEDENT (summary)

Customary mutual conditions including HSR Act expiration without challenge,
delivery of the executed Transition Services Agreement, payoff letters for all
Indebtedness, and delivery of estoppel certificates from the top ten customers
listed on Schedule 8.1(g).

ARTICLE IX — INDEMNIFICATION

9.1 Survival. Representations and warranties survive Closing for eighteen (18)
    months, except fundamental representations which survive for the
    applicable statute of limitations and tax representations which survive
    for seven (7) years.

9.2 Indemnity Cap. Seller's aggregate indemnification obligation (other than
    for fundamental and fraud-based claims) is capped at Ten Million Dollars
    ($10,000,000), of which the first Three Million Dollars ($3,000,000) is
    payable solely from the Indemnity Escrow Amount.

9.3 Basket. No claim may be asserted until aggregate Losses exceed Three
    Hundred Forty Thousand Dollars ($340,000) (the "Basket"), after which
    claims are recoverable from dollar one.

ARTICLE XI — MISCELLANEOUS

11.1 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by Delaware law without
     regard to its conflict-of-laws principles.

11.2 Venue. The Parties consent to exclusive jurisdiction of the Delaware
     Court of Chancery.

11.3 Entire Agreement. This Agreement, together with the Schedules and
     Exhibits, constitutes the entire agreement among the Parties.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the Parties have executed this Agreement as of the Effective
Date.
