RECIPE NOTES — SOURDOUGH STARTER WEEK 2
Logged: Thursday morning

Day 8 update. The starter is finally showing reliable rise — almost doubling
in 6 hours at room temp after the morning feed. Activity feels less aggressive
than the recipe predicted but the smell shifted from "sharp acetone" to
"yogurt + faint apple" which all the forum posts say is the right trajectory.

Feeding ratio for this week:

  Starter:Flour:Water  =  1 : 5 : 5  (by weight)

So this morning: 20g starter, 100g flour (50/50 bread + rye), 100g filtered
water at room temp. Stirred for about a minute and covered with a clean
kitchen towel rather than the airtight lid — needs to breathe.

Plan:
  - Continue 12-hour feeds (morning + evening) through Sunday.
  - First test loaf attempt next Tuesday if the rise stays consistent.
  - If activity stalls, switch to whole wheat flour for 48 hours.

The kitchen ambient has been around 71F which is on the cool end of the
recommended range. Considering moving the jar onto the top of the bread machine
which runs a degree or two warmer. Will try this tonight and note tomorrow.
