EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

On June 26, 2024, the Superior Court of California, San Mateo County, issued
its ruling on Vista's motion for partial summary judgment in Matter
M-2024-0512. The court granted the motion in part and denied it in part.
The ruling materially narrows the scope of Coastal's remaining causes of
action and shifts the bargaining posture in our client's favor for the
remaining counts, though it does not deliver a clear win or loss.

Given the mixed outcome, the Committee's chosen disposition is to pursue
mediated settlement on the surviving counts rather than to either appeal
the partial grant or push the surviving counts to trial. This memo records
that decision and the reasoning underlying it.

THE RULING IN DETAIL

The court's order (Case No. CIV-2024-0118-SM) granted summary judgment in
Vista's favor on Count III (alleged breach of the joint venture exclusivity
clause) on the ground that the relevant exclusivity language was
unambiguously territorial and did not extend to the offshore activity
Coastal challenged. The court denied summary judgment on Counts I and II
(alleged breach of the JV operating agreement royalty provisions and
alleged breach of fiduciary duty by the Vista-nominated JV directors),
holding that material factual disputes precluded judgment as a matter of
law on those counts.

ANALYSIS

This is a mixed outcome — neither a clear favorable nor unfavorable
disposition for Coastal at the current procedural stage. The narrowing of
the case is a strategic shift but not a definitive resolution. Counsel
notes that the ruling does not address damages, settlement amounts, or
final disposition; those questions remain open and will be addressed
through either the recommended mediation or, if mediation fails, through
continued litigation on the surviving counts.

OPTIONS CONSIDERED

The Committee considered three principal options before reaching its
decision:

  Option A — Appeal the Count III grant.  Rejected. The appellate posture
  on contract-interpretation rulings of this kind is weak; California
  appellate courts give substantial deference to trial-court contract
  interpretation when the language is found unambiguous. Estimated
  probability of reversal: 20-25%. Not a productive use of client capital.

  Option B — Push the surviving counts to trial.  Considered but not
  selected. Trial-readiness costs would exceed $480,000 in fees and expert
  expense over an estimated 8-month preparation window. Coastal's
  expressed preference (per meeting with CEO on June 11, 2024) is to
  conclude the matter within calendar year 2024 if commercially possible.

  Option C — Mediated settlement on the surviving counts.  Selected. The
  surviving counts retain economic leverage; Vista's appetite for trial
  on royalty / fiduciary-duty exposure is materially lower than its
  appetite for the exclusivity defense it has just won. Engaging
  experienced mediator Hon. Patricia Yoon (Ret.) in late July 2024 gives
  a realistic 90-day window to reach disposition.
