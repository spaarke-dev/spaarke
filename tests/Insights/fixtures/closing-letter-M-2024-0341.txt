Dear Ms. Lin:

I am writing to confirm the formal closure of Matter M-2024-0341 and to summarize
the disposition reached with opposing counsel at BigFirm LLP.

The matter concluded with terms favorable to our client, securing all material
rights sought in the original complaint. The settlement preserves Acme's exclusive
licensing rights in the North American territory, retains the cure-period clause
in its original 30-day form, and includes a mutual non-disparagement covenant.

Settlement total: $310,000 USD payable within 30 days of execution of the
Settlement and Release Agreement. The first payment of $155,000 was received by
our trust account on August 19, 2024; the balance was received on September 4, 2024
and has been transferred to Acme's operating account net of our final fee invoice.

Final closing on August 15, 2024. The Stipulated Dismissal With Prejudice was
filed in the Northern District of California on the same date (Case No.
3:24-cv-00892-WHO, Dkt. No. 47).

Matter open from January 14, 2024 through closing — 213 days total. This is
materially below our prior IP-licensing matter average against this counterparty
(typically 8-10 months) and reflects the disciplined pre-mediation discovery
posture we adopted in March.

Key terms preserved in the executed settlement:

- Cure-period clause: 30 days (unchanged from original license agreement)
- Exclusive licensing territory: North America (Acme retained as sole licensee)
- Mutual non-disparagement: 5-year survival term
- Settlement payment schedule: two equal installments of $155,000

A copy of the fully-executed Settlement and Release Agreement is enclosed for
your records. We will retain our copy under our standard 7-year matter retention
policy. The matter file has been moved to closed status in our document
management system effective today.
